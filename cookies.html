Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2024

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how ByteWorks UK Ltd uses cookies on this website, and how you can manage cookie settings. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that contain information about your website visits. This policy applies to all cookies used on www.byteworksuk.co.uk and associated pages.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files containing information stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. Cookies are automatically sent to the website server on subsequent visits, allowing the website to remember information about your visit including preferences, login status, and browsing behaviour.

Cookies serve various purposes including:

  • Session Management: Maintaining your login status and session information
  • Personalisation: Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Analytics: Understanding how visitors use the website
  • Performance: Optimising website functionality and loading times
  • Advertising: Displaying relevant advertisements based on browsing history

Cookies may be session cookies (deleted when your browser closes) or persistent cookies (stored on your device until manually deleted or expiration). Cookies may be first-party cookies (set by the website you are visiting) or third-party cookies (set by other domains).

4. Cookie Consent and User Control

Consent Requirements

Under UK PECR and GDPR regulations, websites must obtain user consent before storing or accessing non-essential cookies. Essential cookies are exempt from consent requirements as they are necessary for service delivery. Consent must be obtained through clear opt-in mechanisms before non-essential cookies are stored.

Consent Management

ByteWorks UK Ltd provides a cookie consent mechanism allowing users to accept or decline non-essential cookies. Initial website visits display a cookie consent banner explaining cookie use and providing options to accept all cookies, decline non-essential cookies, or access detailed cookie settings.

Cookie Preferences

Users may adjust cookie preferences by:

  • Interacting with the cookie consent banner on website pages
  • Accessing cookie settings through the website settings page (if available)
  • Modifying browser cookie settings through browser preferences
  • Contacting us at to request changes to cookie preferences

5. Browser Cookie Management

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow users to control cookie storage and deletion through browser settings. You can typically:

  • Block all cookies: Prevent all cookies from being stored on your device
  • Block third-party cookies only: Allow first-party cookies while blocking third-party cookies
  • Allow all cookies: Allow all cookies to be stored
  • Clear existing cookies: Delete cookies previously stored on your device

Browser cookie management options vary by browser type. For detailed instructions, refer to your browser's help documentation or privacy settings.

Cookie Blocking Impact

Blocking cookies may affect website functionality. Essential cookies are necessary for website operation, and blocking these cookies may prevent the website from functioning properly. Non-essential cookies may be declined without affecting core functionality, though some features may be unavailable.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers support Do Not Track (DNT) signals indicating user preference against tracking. ByteWorks UK Ltd respects DNT signals and will not store non-essential cookies where DNT is enabled. However, we cannot guarantee that all third-party services respect DNT signals.

7. Cookie Retention and Deletion

Cookies are retained according to specified durations. Session cookies are automatically deleted when your browser closes. Persistent cookies are retained until the specified expiration date or until manually deleted.
